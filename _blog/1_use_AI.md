Students constantly ask me the same question: *How can I leverage AI as a tool to supercharge my education without accidentally outsourcing my own intelligence?*
So I decided to write my advice down in a succinct post for everyone.

But first a **disclaimer**: AI is evolving at rapid speed, and we still lack replicated, long-term, larger-scale data on its impact. What follows is my personal perspective, backed by the best early research we have. Please take it as a guide, not gospel.

## Motivation: Maximize your Learning
Some of you might be wondering: *Why bother grinding through the tedious process of learning basic computer science skills when AI can already do all my homework assignments in seconds?*
However, state-of-the-art research on real-world tasks shows that **AI is an amplifier of technical skills, not an equalizer** (<abbr title="DORA Team. (2025). State of AI-assisted Software Development 2025. Google Cloud / DORA">DORA 2025</abbr>, <abbr title="Ma, Q., Koedinger, K., & Wu, T. (2026). Not Everyone Wins with LLMs: Behavioral Patterns and Pedagogical Implications for AI Literacy in Programmatic Data Science. Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI '26)">Ma et al. 2026</abbr>, <abbr title="Paradis, E., Grey, K., Madison, Q., Nam, D., Macvean, A., Meimand, V., Zhang, N., Ferrari-Church, B., & Chandra, S. (2025). How much does AI impact development speed? An enterprise-based randomized controlled trial. Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM International Conference on Software Engineering: Software Engineering in Practice (ICSE-SEIP)">Paradis et al. 2025</abbr>). 
When paired with AI, the productivity boost for people with more skills is actually larger (<abbr title="DORA Team. (2025). State of AI-assisted Software Development 2025. Google Cloud / DORA">DORA 2025</abbr>, <abbr title="Ma, Q., Koedinger, K., & Wu, T. (2026). Not Everyone Wins with LLMs: Behavioral Patterns and Pedagogical Implications for AI Literacy in Programmatic Data Science. Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI '26)">Ma et al. 2026</abbr>, <abbr title="Paradis, E., Grey, K., Madison, Q., Nam, D., Macvean, A., Meimand, V., Zhang, N., Ferrari-Church, B., & Chandra, S. (2025). How much does AI impact development speed? An enterprise-based randomized controlled trial. Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM International Conference on Software Engineering: Software Engineering in Practice (ICSE-SEIP)">Paradis et al. 2025</abbr>). 
The research tells a clear story: AI benefits those with experience and strong foundational skills much more than those who offload 

This means that **as a college student, your main goal should be to  maximize your skills** so that, when you then add AI on top, you amplify a larger base of skills, and you also increase the amplification factor.
Unfortunately, AI as a technology often incentivizes behavior that reduces skill formation, if used the wrong way. 
On the other hand, if used correctly, it has the potential to rapidly accelerate the learning journey of students who use AI to cognitively challenge themselves more, not less.

This blog post is intended to be a guide for students who are trying to elevate their learning journey to be well prepared for a world in which AI is potentially increasingly replacing cognitive work and the bar we need to reach might be rising more and more with every release of more capable models.
